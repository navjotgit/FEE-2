Review: The Great Gatsby

Author:F. Scott Fitzgerald

Review:A classic novel of the Roaring Twenties, The Great Gatsby captures the extravagance, idealism, and social upheaval of the era. The story revolves around Jay Gatsby, a wealthy man with a mysterious past, and his obsessive love for Daisy Buchanan. Fitzgerald’s writing is beautiful and evocative, making this novel a must-read.

Review: 1984

Author: George Orwell

Review:George Orwell’s dystopian novel 1984 is a chilling depiction of a totalitarian regime where the government controls every aspect of life. The book explores themes of surveillance, censorship, and individualism in a world where Big Brother is always watching. It's a thought-provoking read that remains relevant today.

Review: To Kill a Mockingbird

Author: Harper Lee

Review:Set in the American South during the 1930s, To Kill a Mockingbird is a powerful narrative about race, justice, and morality. Through the eyes of young Scout Finch, we witness the trial of a black man falsely accused of raping a white woman. Harper Lee’s novel is both a poignant coming-of-age story and a scathing critique of racial injustice.
